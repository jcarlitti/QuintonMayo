About Me

 Quinton Mayo walked onto the campus of Randolph-Macon College in 2015 with the intent to graduate in four years with a degree in Computer Science. After one year of using the lab computers to compare John Wall's prime to that of hall-of-famer, Isiah Thomas, it was clear his heart wasn't in PHP, Java, or HTML, but sports analysis.
 Soon after, Quinton switched his major pursuit to Communication Studies with a minor in Journalism -- a program that was extremely underdeveloped at the school. With the goal of proving to his parents, and himself, that a future in sports television was the right move, Quinton worked tirelessly to grow the journalism program at R-MC, maximize resources and opportunities on the campus, and set himself up for a career in the beloved field come graduation in May, 2019.
 January 2017 marks the month in which Quinton started his sports analysis youtube channel "Mayo Please." His first video was focused on breaking down the game of a young basketball prospect out of Salisbury, North Carolina by the name of Zion Williamson. Quinton filmed, edited, and published the video on YouTube -- 66,000+ views later, he knew he had made the right decision. The sheer volume of videos Quinton produced, while maintaining a 3.8+ GPA and playing on the Yellow-Jackets football team, caused him to come to a stark reality: "If these are the results of my efforts right now, with all these different things on my plate, what would be the result of even more focus on developing as a sports analyst?"
 Quinton decided to step down from the R-MC football team and fully commit any and all free time to the development of his career path -- which really never felt like a "career," but genuine fun during spare time. He began working for the R-MC Yellow Jacket school newspaper as a junior sports reporter covering a variety of sports from field hockey to the Equestrian Club. The on campus involvement pursuit didn't stop there as Quinton was then selected as the play-by-play and color commentator for the R-MC baseball team -- a season in which they finished 38-9-1 and lost in the Division III World Series. R-MC hadn't had a baseball commentator, since Quinton, in recent program history.
 During summer of 2017 Quinton elected to stay in Ashland, VA, rather than go home to live in Maryland, and intern with WTVR-TV CBS 6 in Richmond, VA. Because the internship was unpaid, and housing on the campus of R-MC cost, Quinton worked part time at Food Lion, as well as continued to produce sports analysis videos via YouTube, in order to pay for rent and food. That summer, Quinton also started the company "Mayo Please," which features a team of 10 talented aspiring full time journalists contributing written and digital content covering the four major sports leagues.
 After a grueling summer, school returned to session and Quinton kept the same responsibilities but felt it was necessary to add even more to his plate. He joined the "Locked on Wizards podcast," in which he quickly put his signature on through extensive basketball comprehension, Washington Wizards knowledge, powerful personality, podcast presence, as well as video and audio editing prowess. If unable to tell so far, Quinton loved the idea of developing the campus of R-MC to assist those who come after him in channeling their love for broadcast journalism. Therefore, he became President of the R-MC radio station, Stinger Radio, and flipped it upside down with new software, shows, music selection, and a new location on campus.
 The summer of 2018, Quinton became the first student in R-MC history to secure an internship with WJLA ABC 7/News Channel 8 in Arlington, VA. While the internship was unpaid and for academic credit (which is determined by an hour threshold) Quinton was so dedicated to the position, and passionate about the DMV sports scene, that he completed the hours needed in month one. For the next two months he continued volunteering at the same rate, because there was nothing else in the world he'd rather do. Duh. Quinton completed interviews and participated in introductory press conferences with Dwight Howard, Troy Brown Jr., Jeff Green, Austin Rivers, Scott Brooks, Ernie Grunfeld, Ted Leonsis, Nicklas Bäckström, Tiger Woods, Todd Reirden, Ricky Fowler, Billy Horschel, Damon Evans, Jay Gruden, and Ryan Kerrigan, Josh Norman, Paul Richardson, Vernon Davis, and many others.
 Senior year, same story. Quinton added another Infinity Stone to his Gauntlet -- the Richmond Times Dispatch. This opportunity gave him another taste of what it's like to meet deadlines, search for stories, work effectively in an office setting, multi-task, and produce compelling and informative content -- all skills in which he currently holds and is continuously mastering every single day. At the end of his senior year, Quinton released a video focusing on the perspective of Wizards fans, which went viral on Twitter. The video found the right set of eyes and NBC Sports Washington brought him onto the digital team.
 Quinton's first few months at NBC Sports Washington were spent as a digital producer. This job included a multitude of responsibilities such as writing and editing articles, managing social media accounts, optimizing content for the website as well as the app, and mining stories. With the Washington Wizards season quickly approaching, the company was looking for a host, alongside Travis Thomas, that shared a passion and knowledge for basketball, as well as camera presence and charisma. On October 2nd, NBC Sports Washington and the Washington Wizards announced Thomas and Mayo as the hosts of Wizards Outsiders.
 In this current role Quinton hosts the pregame show "Wizards Outsiders,", and postgame show "Wizards Talk," as well as the Wizards Talk podcast. Every game, Quinton breaks down every play and creates a package for every fan to receive quality analysis of why the Wizards won or lost. Oh, and it's without saying Quinton takes you on a roller coaster of emotions while watching. No matter the background, interest, or situation, Quinton connects to the fans in a way that not many anchors can. With his first five months of television experience under his belt, it's fair to say that if he's already "good," great is on the horizon.

Mayo Please Network

Boston & Brute

A partnership that formed on the campus of Randolph-Macon College, Jake Jones and Quinton Mayo go back-and-forth with basketball and football analysis. Jones' Boston Bias always seems to rear its head while Mayo's love for everything DMV overpowers all ... well until, they start talking championships.

The Flock Podcast

Hosted by Quinton Mayo and his co-host Jaire Perry, Chris McMillian and Jacoby Freeman. A safe haven for these four brothers to talk about their journey through life.

Quinton Mayo Show

A haven for Mayo to talk all sports, this has largely worked as a Washington WIzards' confession space. Bringing an "OT" period to the work he does on NBC Sports Washington Wizards Outsiders and Wizards Talk, this podcast is long form and wide ranging through the utilization of Mayo's sources, fine-tuned analysis, and contriving commentary.

Kirby on Sports

You want passion, Josh Kirby brings it. Kirby delivers a weekly podcast centered around D.C. sports but also takes on league wide topics across the four major sports. Kirby is known for his elite guests and conversational skills.

In the Field

A Redskins centric podcast hosted by John Bird, Jordan Miles and Parker Miles. The team is brutally honest about whats going on in Landver, MD each sunday while delivering an easily consumable analysis each week. No cut cards, just edgy commentary supported by facts ... sometimes.

Hooop and the Harm

Hosted by Matt Nguyen, this pod focuses mainly on injuries around the association and how they impact your fantasy lineups. Nguyen is a certified Athletic Trainer based out of Houston, Texas.
